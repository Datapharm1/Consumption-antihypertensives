**Topic**
-
Prescription Pattern and Patient Consumption of Antihypertensive Medications in a Government Funded Free Clinic in Nigeria

**Aim**
-
To assess the prescribing patterns, dispensing frequency, and consumption of antihypertensive medications to inform drug forecasting, enhance monitoring practices, and optimise resource allocation.

**Specific objectives**
-
- To describe the demographic characteristics( age, gender) of patients receiving antihypertensive medications during the study period (Feb.2024 â€“ September 2024)
- To evaluate the types and strengths of antihypertensive drugs prescribed and dispensed during the study period, and to identify the most frequently prescribed antihypertensive drugs.
- To identify cases of early refills for each antihypertensive medications and determine the amount lost due to early refills
- To determine the consumption rate of the antihypertensive medications in the clinic
- To provide recommendations based on findings

**Procedure**
-
- The dispensing data of antihypertensives from February 2024 to September, 2024 was abstracted from the record book and entered into a spreadsheet.
- Information entered- Personal number, Gender, Age, Date of dispensing, Antihypertensives dispensed and the quantity( 0.5 for 2 weeks, 1 for one month, 2 for two months and so on)
- The data was cleaned and analyzed using Microsoft Excel and Python packages for data and statistical analysis (Numpy, Pandas, Scipy, Matplotlib)
